Key Events — 2020 to 2025
Major disruptions and their direct impact on French maritime supply chains
2020
COVID-19 Global Shock
Global trade collapsed. French energy imports fell 16.2% year-on-year. Port operations severely disrupted across all major hubs.
Energy imports: −16.2% YoY (SDES real data)
2022
Russia–Ukraine War — European Energy Crisis
Europe replaced Russian pipeline gas. France became Europe's primary LNG gateway. Dunkerque and Marseille reached peak utilization. French energy imports surged +17.2%.
Energy imports: +17.2% YoY — France became EU top LNG entry point
2023–2024
Red Sea Crisis — Houthi Attacks on Shipping
Major carriers rerouted via Cape of Good Hope (+10–14 days). France–Asia volumes fell 50%. Marseille lost 7.1% of shipping links. Spain gained 13% maritime cost advantage over French ports.
France–Asia trade: −50% / Marseille connections: −7.1% (Bedoya-Maya et al., 2025)
2024
Worst French Cereal Harvest in 40 Years
Excessive rainfall. France's worst harvest since the 1980s. Yet HAROPA maintained 7.04 Mt cereal exports (78.8% of max) — demonstrating strong logistical resilience under production stress.
HAROPA exports held at 7.04 Mt despite −40% production drop (SDES real data)
2025
Trade Uncertainty and Tariff Pressures
Global tariff tensions reduced shipments. French energy imports continued declining (−3.7%). Agri-food throughput turned negative for the first time since 2020.
Energy imports: −3.7% YoY (SDES 2024)

Strategic Implications

Recommendations

What should policymakers and port authorities do to improve French maritime supply chain resilience?

1
Develop Atlantic Energy Gateway Capacity
France's bipolar energy structure (Dunkerque + Marseille) leaves no buffer under disruption. Expanding LNG terminal capacity on the Atlantic façade — Nantes Saint-Nazaire, La Rochelle — would provide a third gateway and reduce catastrophic single-point failure risk. All current terminals are fully booked under CRE long-term contracts.
Energy
2
Increase Strategic LNG Storage Capacity
Expanding underground gas storage and LNG buffer tanks would allow France to absorb 2–4 week supply shocks without triggering port congestion. Currently, 100% terminal bookings leave zero spot capacity for emergency imports.
Energy
3
Strengthen HAROPA Cereal Export Redundancy
HAROPA runs at 78.8% for cereals and handles 52.2% of national exports. Developing La Rochelle (68.2%) and Dunkerque (41.8%) as complementary gateways reduces single-hub dependency. The 2024 harvest shock showed how close to the operational limit the system runs.
Agri-Food
4
Diversify Energy Import Partners
France imports ~78% of LNG from the USA. While this reduced Russian gas dependency post-2022, it creates a new concentration risk. Diversifying toward Norway, Qatar, and Africa reduces the Chokepoint Dependency Ratio.
Energy
5
Implement Real-Time KPI Monitoring Dashboard
This platform is the operational prototype. Port authorities lack a unified, real-time system to track capacity utilization across sectors. A live KPI dashboard updated quarterly with SDES data enables early-warning detection and coordinated crisis response.
Both
6
Invest in Port Cybersecurity Infrastructure
44% of maritime professionals have faced cyber threats. A PCS attack reduces throughput to 30% instantly. Resilient, redundant digital systems and formal incident response plans are critical infrastructure protection.
Both

KPI Glossary

Definition, formula, and interpretation for every KPI in this dashboard.

Capacity Utilization Ratio
CUR = T_2024 / max(T_2000–2024)
Measures how intensively a port is used for a given sector. Proxy = max historical throughput. Below 70% = comfortable, 70–85% = sensitive, above 85% = high risk. Source: SDES.
Throughput Growth
Growth = (T_t − T_{t-1}) / T_{t-1}
Year-over-year change in port traffic. Measures competitive attractiveness of French ports over time. Source: SDES / UN Comtrade.
EU Market Share
MS = Throughput_FR / Throughput_EU
Share of French ports in total EU throughput by sector. Measures France's competitive position versus peers. Source: Eurostat.
Geographic Diversification
HHI = Σ(Share_basin)²
Herfindahl-Hirschman Index across maritime basins (Channel, Atlantic, Mediterranean, Overseas). Lower HHI = more diversified = more resilient. Source: SDES.
Chokepoint Dependency
CDR = Chokepoint_flows / Total_flows
Proportion of trade transiting strategic maritime chokepoints. High CDR = high vulnerability to single-point disruptions. Source: UN Comtrade / Ang (2021).
Port Dependency Ratio
PDR = Top-k ports / National total
Concentration of strategic commodity flows through a limited number of ports. High PDR = high structural risk. Source: SDES.
Port Connectivity Score
PCS = LSCI (UNCTAD)
Liner Shipping Connectivity Index. France's LSCI declined from ~290 (2017) to ~260 (2024). Source: UNCTAD.
Delay Rate (MTDR)
MTDR = Disruption_days / Total_days
Share of days with significant operational disruption. Le Havre reliability score: 2.4 vs Antwerp 4.5. Source: Wendler-Bosco & Nicholson (2020).

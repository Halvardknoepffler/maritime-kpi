Key Events — 2020 to 2026
Major disruptions and their direct impact on French maritime supply chains
2020
COVID-19 Global Shock
Global trade collapsed. French energy imports fell 16.2% year-on-year.
Energy imports: −16.2% YoY (SDES real data)
2022
Russia–Ukraine War — European Energy Crisis
France became Europe's primary LNG gateway. Dunkerque and Marseille reached peak utilization. French energy imports surged +17.2%.
Energy imports: +17.2% YoY — France became EU top LNG entry point
2023–2024
Red Sea Crisis — Houthi Attacks on Shipping
Major carriers rerouted via Cape of Good Hope (+10–14 days). France–Asia volumes fell 50%. Marseille lost 7.1% of shipping links. Spain gained 13% maritime cost advantage.
France–Asia trade: −50% / Marseille connections: −7.1% (Bedoya-Maya et al., 2025)
2024
Worst French Cereal Harvest in 40 Years
Excessive rainfall. France's worst harvest since the 1980s. Yet HAROPA maintained 7.04 Mt cereal exports — demonstrating strong logistical resilience under production stress.
HAROPA exports held at 7.04 Mt despite −40% production drop (SDES real data)
Feb 24, 2026
Strait of Hormuz Near-Total Closure — Iran Crisis
Escalating Iran–US tensions led to near-total closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Tanker traffic fell 90%. 34% of France's total energy imports transit this chokepoint. TotalEnergies holds a major stake in Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG complex — now offline.
Tanker traffic: −90% · EU gas prices: +70% · Brent crude: $103/bbl → rising
Mar 2, 2026
Qatar Ras Laffan LNG Complex Struck
Qatar's Ras Laffan complex — which supplies ~10% of EU LNG imports — was struck and went offline. TotalEnergies' stake directly blocks LNG production and revenues. France holds long-term Qatari LNG contracts now at risk.
TotalEnergies Ras Laffan stake: offline since March 2026
Mar 25, 2026
IEA Emergency Reserve Release
International Energy Agency released 400 million barrels of strategic reserves to stabilize markets. France activated national gas storage protocols.
IEA: 400M bbl reserves released · EU gas price surge: +70%
Apr 8, 2026
Ceasefire Agreed — Strait Still Constrained
A ceasefire was agreed but the Strait of Hormuz remains partially constrained. War risk insurance premiums remain elevated. France's debt-to-GDP at 115% limits fiscal capacity to absorb further shocks.
France debt/GDP: 115% · Little fiscal room to absorb prolonged shock

Geographic Diversification Index

How traffic in a given sector is distributed across France's main maritime basins — lower HHI = more resilient.

HHI by Maritime Basin — 2015–2024
Channel (MCH) · Mediterranean (MED) · Atlantic (ATL) · Source: SDES Statinfo
Channel concentration has decreased since 2019 — a positive signal. Atlantic basin is growing (La Rochelle, Nantes). Mediterranean remains stable. Overall diversification is improving but Channel and Mediterranean still dominate.
Formula
Share_b,s,t = T_b,s,t / ΣT_b,s,t  ·  HHI_s,t = Σ(Share_b,s,t)²  ·  Diversification = 1 − HHI
Lower HHI = more diversified = more resilient. Source: SDES Statinfo Ports Maritimes · Eurostat
Strength KPI

Sectoral Port Connectivity Score

How well connected French ports are to other ports in a given sector — based on the UNCTAD Liner Shipping Connectivity Index (LSCI).

LSCI — France vs European Peers
Liner Shipping Connectivity Index · Source: UNCTAD
France's LSCI has declined from ~290 (2017) to ~260 (2024). Major carriers are concentrating on hub ports (Rotterdam, Antwerp) and reducing direct calls to French ports. Spain gained a 13% maritime cost advantage post-Red Sea crisis, partly due to better connectivity scores.
What LSCI measures
Number of vessels, shipping companies, liner services, container ship size, and maximum vessel size. A declining LSCI means fewer direct shipping connections — importers and exporters face higher costs and longer transit times.
Formula
Connectivity_p,s,t = N_partners_p,s,t (number of distinct partner ports served). At national level, France uses UNCTAD LSCI as proxy. Source: UNCTAD · Merk et al. (2011)

PCR_k,s,t = Σ(T_p,s,t) / Σ(T_p,s,t) for top-k ports

Overseas Dependency Ratio (ODR) per Port

ODR = Total exports / Total imports. ODR > 1 = export-oriented (resilient). ODR < 1 = import-dependent (vulnerable). Source: UN Comtrade.

Recommendations

Global recommendations from our group based on KPI analysis and what-if scenario findings.

1
Reduce Reliance on Strategic Chokepoints
Develop alternative routing strategies: Suez blockage → Cape of Good Hope (+10–12 days). Strait of Malacca → Sunda & Lombok Straits. Strait of Hormuz → Habshan-Fujairah pipeline (1.8 Mb/d) or Saudi East-West pipeline (5 Mb/d).
Both
2
Strengthen Port Connectivity & Competitiveness
Implement competitive port dues with volume discounts, loyalty pricing, and green vessel incentives. Extend operating hours and adopt automated equipment to cut dwell time. Efficient operations are essential as port dues alone are insufficient to attract shipping lines.
Both
3
Balance Flows Across Maritime Basins
Invest in small port infrastructure — Port La Nouvelle as viable alternative to Marseille-Fos. Create port specialisation: Sète for agri-food, Toulon for cruises, Marseille for general cargo. Offer better pricing incentives at smaller ports to redistribute Mediterranean cargo traffic.
Both
4
Develop Atlantic Energy Gateway Capacity
France's bipolar energy structure (Dunkerque + Marseille) leaves no buffer under disruption. Expanding LNG terminal capacity on the Atlantic façade — Nantes Saint-Nazaire, La Rochelle — provides a third gateway. All current terminals are fully booked under CRE long-term contracts.
Energy
5
Increase Strategic LNG Storage Capacity
100% terminal bookings leave zero spot capacity for emergency imports. Expanding underground gas storage and LNG buffer tanks would allow France to absorb 2–4 week supply shocks. Critical given France's 115% debt-to-GDP limiting fiscal response capacity.
Energy
6
Strengthen HAROPA Cereal Export Redundancy
HAROPA runs at 78.8% and handles 52.2% of national exports. Developing La Rochelle (68.2%) and Dunkerque (41.8%) as complementary gateways reduces single-hub dependency exposed by the 2024 harvest shock.
Agri-Food
7
Implement Real-Time KPI Monitoring Dashboard
This platform is the operational prototype. Port authorities lack a unified, real-time system to track capacity utilization across sectors. Live KPI monitoring updated quarterly with SDES data enables early-warning detection and coordinated crisis response.
Both

KPI Glossary

Definition, formula, and interpretation for every KPI in this dashboard.

Capacity Utilization Ratio
CUR = T_2024 / max(T_2000–2024)
Measures how intensively a port is used. Proxy = max historical throughput. Below 70% = comfortable, 70–85% = sensitive, above 85% = high risk. Source: SDES.
Throughput Growth
Growth = (T_t − T_{t-1}) / T_{t-1}
Year-over-year change in port traffic. Measures competitive attractiveness of French ports. Source: SDES / UN Comtrade.
EU Market Share
MS = Throughput_FR / Throughput_EU
Share of French ports in total EU throughput. Source: Eurostat.
Geographic Diversification
HHI = Σ(Share_basin)²
Herfindahl-Hirschman Index across maritime basins. Lower HHI = more resilient. Source: SDES.
Chokepoint Dependency
CDR = Chokepoint_flows / Total_flows
Proportion of trade transiting strategic chokepoints. High CDR = high systemic vulnerability. Source: UN Comtrade / Ang (2021).
Port Dependency Ratio
PDR = Top-k ports / National total
Concentration of commodity flows through a limited number of ports. Source: SDES.
Port Connectivity Score
PCS = LSCI (UNCTAD)
Liner Shipping Connectivity Index. France declined from ~290 (2017) to ~260 (2024). Source: UNCTAD.
Delay Rate (MTDR)
MTDR = Disruption_days / Total_days
Share of days with significant operational disruption. Le Havre 2.4 vs Antwerp 4.5. Source: Wendler-Bosco (2020).
Overseas Dependency Ratio (ODR)
ODR = Total_exports_port / Total_imports_port
Measures structural dependence of a port by comparing export and import values. ODR > 1.0 = export-oriented (resilient). ODR < 1.0 = import-dependent (vulnerable). ODR ≈ 1.0 = balanced. Source: UN Comtrade.
